About EEE
The department of Electrical & Electronics was established in the year 1994 with an annual student intake of 60 in B.E.(EEE) course. Presently, the department offers a 4- year undergraduate program leading to B.Tech degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering of JNTU Kakinada with an annual intake of 120 students. A P.G. program M.Tech. (Power Systems and Automation) was started in the year 2010 and currently the annual intake is 6. The department has experienced faculty and excellent infrastructural facilities in terms of laboratories, space and computer systems. The Department of Electrical & Electronics Engineering has been accredited TWICE by National Board of Accreditation (NBA), New Delhi. The Department is recognized as Centre for Research by Andhra University, Visakhapatnam and JNTU, Kakinada and ‘ONE’ of our faculty member recognized Research Co-Supervisor, he is guiding “2” research scholars registered in our research centre. The department promotes research and development in thrust areas like Power System Optimization, Power System Protection, Micro Grid, Load frequency control, Renewable energy, etc...

Why EEE ?
EEE has numerous applications in various fields and industries. Firstly, in the energy sector, EEE plays a vital role in power generation, transmission, and distribution systems. It involves designing and maintaining electrical grids, renewable energy systems, and smart grids for efficient energy management. Secondly, EEE is crucial in the field of telecommunications, enabling the design and development of communication networks, mobile devices, and satellite systems. Additionally, EEE is essential in the development of electronic devices and systems, including computers, smartphones, medical equipment, and consumer electronics. Lastly, EEE is employed in automation and control systems, enabling the design and implementation of robotics, industrial automation, and process control systems in manufacturing and other industries. Overall, EEE's applications encompass power systems, telecommunications, electronics, and automation, contributing to advancements in various sectors of modern society.
